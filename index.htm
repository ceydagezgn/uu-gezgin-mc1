Questions

 

1 � Design and develop visualizations and visual analytic tools that will allow Silas to explore and understand the profile of Sailor Shift�s career. 

a.      Who has she been most influenced by over time? 

This question is addressed as part of an integrated arc diagram, which visually combines all three sub-questions of Task 1 into a single framework.

To determine who influenced Sailor Shift over time, we first identified all musical works in which she took part as a performer, lyricist, composer, or producer. For each of these works, we then examined whether it was connected to earlier works through inspiration-based edges such as InStyleOf, CoverOf, InterpolatesFrom, DirectlySamples, or LyricalReferenceTo. Only valid chronological relationships—where the influencing work was released prior to Sailor’s—were considered.

Next, we extracted the creative contributors of these earlier works. Each contributor was counted once per creative role (e.g., both performer and producer roles count as two distinct influences). This enabled us to compute how many times each artist influenced Sailor and in which years those influences occurred.

In the arc diagram, influence relationships appear on the left side of Sailor Shift's node. Arc thickness and bar height represent how often each artist influenced her. Nodes are chronologically ordered by first influence year. Tooltips show basic details, while time and frequency filters let users explore specific periods and highlight stronger influences.

As a result, seven artists were found to have influenced Sailor four or more times between 2024–2040, with Gang Shao being the earliest and Qiang Liu the most recent. These patterns suggest that Sailor’s inspirations shifted over time, often shaped by short-term, context-specific interactions.

b.      Who has she collaborated with and directly or indirectly influenced? 

This question focuses on Sailor Shift’s direct and indirect collaborations, exploring how her creative influence might have extended through chains of artistic cooperation. To answer this, we first identified all works in which Sailor took on a creative role. Artists who co-created with her in the same year and on the same work were labeled as Level 1 collaborators. From this group, a second set—Level 2 collaborators—was identified. These are artists who worked with Level 1 collaborators in later years, without ever having worked directly with Sailor themselves. This methodology allowed for the tracing of Sailor's influence beyond her immediate artistic circle.

In the visualization, collaboration patterns are displayed to the right of the central Sailor Shift node. Green arcs denote direct (Level 1), and yellow arcs indicate indirect (Level 2) collaborators. The thickness of each arc and the height of the accompanying bar represent collaboration frequency. Nodes are ordered chronologically based on the initial year of collaboration. Furthermore, the time filter above and the collaboration threshold filter below enable users to isolate specific periods or focus on more prominent artistic relationships. This layered structure not only reveals Sailor's collaborative reach but also distinguishes between her direct influence (on Level 1 collaborators) and her indirect influence (on Level 2 collaborators who were likely exposed to her artistic style through shared creative networks).

As a result, the most frequent was Maya Jensen, who worked with Sailor four times across different years — 2024, 2025, 2026, and 2037. This makes her the only artist with such a sustained collaborative relationship over time. Maya's repeated involvement, spread across more than a decade, highlights a strong and lasting artistic connection.

c.       How has she influenced collaborators of the broader Oceanus Folk community? 

This question focuses on understanding how Sailor Shift influenced the creative output of her collaborators within the Oceanus Folk genre. The analysis includes both Level 1 and Level 2 collaborators identified in earlier steps. For each artist, we defined two distinct periods: before and after their first collaboration with Sailor. In each period, we calculated the number of Oceanus Folk works they participated in, as well as how many of those were marked as notable.

The aim was to assess whether there was a measurable increase in Oceanus Folk involvement following collaboration with Sailor. If both the total number of Oceanus Folk works and the number of notable works increased after the collaboration year, the artist was considered to be positively influenced by her.

In the arc diagram visualization, this effect is represented by the color of the collaborator nodes on the right side. Purple nodes indicate that the artist experienced an increase in both Oceanus Folk and notable works after working with Sailor, while gray nodes show no such increase. This binary color coding helps users immediately recognize where Sailor’s influence may have had a lasting impact.When hovering over each node, a tooltip appears summarizing whether an increase was observed.

As a result, this design makes it possible to quickly evaluate the broader ripple effects of Sailor’s collaborations, helping to reveal her role in elevating Oceanus Folk productivity among associated artists.

2 � Develop visualizations that illustrate how the influence of Oceanus Folk has spread through the musical world. 

a.      Was this influence intermittent or did it have a gradual rise? 

To explore how the influence of Oceanus Folk evolved over time, we first identified all works in the dataset labeled with the “Oceanus Folk” genre. Using the influence-based edges in the graph — including DirectlySamples, InterpolatesFrom, CoverOf, LyricalReferenceTo, and InStyleOf — we determined which works were influenced by Oceanus Folk.

A key condition was that only works released after an Oceanus Folk work could be considered influenced by it. This temporal filtering ensured that the influence relationship respected chronological order.

The original dataset includes 16 sub-genres influenced by Oceanus Folk. To enhance readability and reduce visual clutter, we grouped these sub-genres into six major genre categories: Pop, Rock, Metal, Folk, Electronic, and Punk. For instance, sub-genres such as “Dream Pop”, “Indie Pop”, and “Synthpop” were consolidated under the broader “Pop” category. This grouping strategy was consistently applied across all visualizations to allow for more intuitive comparison and interpretation.

To visualize this dynamic, we developed an interactive stacked area chart that shows the number of influenced works per year, broken down by major genre groups. Each colored layer in the graph corresponds to a genre group, while the black dashed line displays the yearly production of original Oceanus Folk works. Users can hover over points on the chart to view tooltips with year-specific and genre-specific totals.

As a result, the findings suggest that the influence of Oceanus Folk has been intermittent, rather than following a gradual rise. After a production peak around 2023, a marked increase in influenced works occurred between 2025 and 2031, indicating episodic surges in impact rather than continuous growth.

b.      What genres and top artists have been most influenced by Oceanus Folk? 

To address this question, we first utilized the set of works previously identified as being influenced by the Oceanus Folk genre. From these influenced works, we extracted the contributing artists by examining four key edge types: PerformerOf, ComposerOf, ProducerOf, and LyricistOf. This process ensured that we captured all creators who played a role in the development of those influenced works.

To identify the most influenced artists, we calculated the frequency with which each artist participated in such works. Artists with higher participation counts were considered more influenced by Oceanus Folk. To maintain clarity in the visualization, only artists who contributed to three or more influenced works were included, resulting in a focused group of 45 artists.

The corresponding visualization is a Sankey diagram that displays how Oceanus Folk has influenced both genre groups and individual artists. Although the dataset includes many subgenres, they were grouped into six main categories to enhance readability. On the right side of the diagram, two panels are provided: the upper panel lists the top 11 most influenced artists, and the lower panel reveals detailed information about each artist when their name is selected.

As a result, Rock emerges as the genre most influenced by Oceanus Folk, with 51 associated works, followed by Folk with 40. At the artist level, Blazing Collective stands out, contributing five times across both Rock and Folk works in roles such as performer, composer, and producer. This illustrates Oceanus Folk’s cross-genre impact on modern music and creators.

c.       On the converse, how has Oceanus Folk changed with the rise of Sailor Shift? From which genres does it draw most of its contemporary inspiration? 

To investigate how Oceanus Folk has changed over time and from which genres it draws its contemporary inspiration, we conducted a two-part analysis.

First, all works categorized under the "Oceanus Folk" genre were identified. Among these, those in which Sailor Shift played a creative role were separately counted. This allowed us to compare the overall yearly production in the Oceanus Folk genre with Sailor’s individual contributions.

Second, we analyzed the inspirational sources behind Oceanus Folk works. By examining influence-based edges and considering only source works released prior to the Oceanus Folk creations, we identified relevant influences. The genres of these source works were then extracted to assess the genre influences shaping Oceanus Folk in recent years.

To communicate these insights, we designed two synchronized visualizations. The first is a stacked area chart that displays the genre-based inspirations behind Oceanus Folk across time. The second is a dual-line chart comparing the total number of Oceanus Folk works per year with the number that featured Sailor Shift. Both visuals include interactive tooltips and genre group color coding.

The visualization shows that the primary source of inspiration for Oceanus Folk has been the Folk genre. Oceanus Folk production peaked in 2023, and Sailor Shift began contributing in 2024 — likely influenced by this surge in popularity. Despite a decline in overall output afterward, Sailor continued creating works in this genre almost every year. From 2028 onward, her contributions became consistent, and by 2040, she was the sole artist producing Oceanus Folk works. This suggests that although Sailor entered the genre during its peak, she later became central to preserving and sustaining its presence in the musical landscape.

3 � Use your visualizations to develop a profile of what it means to be a rising star in the music industry. 

a.      Visualize the careers of three artists. Compare and contrast their rise in popularity and influence. 

To explore what it means to be a rising star in the music industry, this analysis focused on three artists: Maya Jensen, Sophie Ramirez, and Lila “Lilly” Hartman. These artists were selected because of their shared history with Sailor Shift. All three were members of the same music group as Sailor and worked together for three years, making them suitable subjects for comparative analysis based on similar starting conditions.

The comparative evaluation was carried out using a customized parallel coordinates plot that displays each artist’s values across ten numerical features. These include metrics such as number of unique works, role distribution, collaboration count, genre diversity, and notable work involvement. The definitions of these features and the normalization process used to prepare the data will be explained in the following prediction question.

Among the three, Maya Jensen stands out with the strongest profile—especially in prominent roles and sustained recent activity. Sophie Ramirez exhibits similar performance but surpasses Maya in terms of unique collaborators, reflecting a broader professional network. In contrast, Lila Hartman trails behind in almost all dimensions, particularly in recent activity. While Maya and Sophie show strong potential as emerging figures within the Oceanus Folk scene, Lila’s limited recent output and lower scores indicate a weaker trajectory moving forward.

b.     Using this characterization, give three predictions of who the next Oceanus Folk stars with be over the next five years.  

This question aims to identify the top three artists most likely to become future stars in the Oceanus Folk genre over the next five years. The prediction is based on a custom feature-based model specifically designed for this task. Only artists who have participated in at least one Oceanus Folk work were included, as prior involvement in the genre is considered a necessary criterion for future prominence.

Ten normalized features were used in the model: unique works, role score, recent work count (last 5 years), notable work count, notable role score, recent notable works (last 5 years), genre diversity, Oceanus Folk ratio, unique collaborators, and inspired works. Users can assign custom weights to these features using the interactive dashboard, allowing for dynamic ranking of artists based on different definitions of star potential.

Although the Elbow method suggested that the optimal number of clusters might be three, a four-cluster configuration was ultimately chosen. In the three-cluster setup, Sailor Shift — the current leading star — was grouped with other artists, making it difficult to distinguish her uniquely strong profile. In contrast, the four-cluster configuration clearly separated her into a distinct group, reinforcing her outlier status in terms of productivity, influence, and genre commitment. The slightly higher Silhouette score for four clusters also supported this choice, indicating better-defined clusters.
